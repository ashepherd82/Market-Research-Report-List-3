<p><p>Porcine Epidemic Diarrhea (PED) vaccines are classified into two main types: live vaccines and killed vaccines. Live vaccines contain weakened or attenuated forms of the virus, stimulating a strong immune response without causing disease. Killed vaccines, on the other hand, contain inactivated virus particles, which also trigger an immune response but do not cause infection. Both types of vaccines play a crucial role in preventing and controlling the spread of PED virus in pigs, ultimately contributing to improved animal health and productivity.</p></p>
