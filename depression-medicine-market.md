<p><p>The depression medicine market can be categorized into various types. Tricyclic antidepressants work by increasing the levels of certain chemicals in the brain. Serotonin-norepinephrine inhibitors also affect the levels of these chemicals, but in a different way. Atypical antipsychotics are commonly used alongside antidepressants to treat depression. Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors help elevate serotonin levels in the brain. Benzodiazepines are mainly prescribed for short-term relief of anxiety and insomnia. Tetracyclic antidepressants work by increasing the levels of norepinephrine in the brain. Monoamine oxidase inhibitors prevent the breakdown of certain chemicals involved in regulating mood. Finally, there are other drugs available in the market that are used to treat depression.</p></p>
