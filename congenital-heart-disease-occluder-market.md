<p><p>The Congenital Heart Disease Occluder Market includes three main types: Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA), Atrial Septal Defects (ASD), and Ventricular Septal Defects (VSD). PDA occurs when a blood vessel fails to close after birth, causing an abnormal blood flow. ASD refers to a hole in the wall between the heart's upper chambers, while VSD is a hole in the wall separating the heart's lower chambers. These occluders are medical devices used to close or repair these defects, allowing for improved cardiac function and reducing the risk of complications.</p></p>
