<p><p>Blood component extractors are medical devices used in blood banks and hospitals to separate whole blood into its individual components such as red blood cells, platelets, and plasma. Intermittent type extractors are designed to separate blood into components in batches, allowing manual intervention at each stage. Continuous type extractors, on the other hand, automate the separation process without requiring manual intervention, enabling a continuous flow of blood and efficient collection of components. Both types of extractors play a crucial role in ensuring a steady supply of blood components for transfusions and other medical procedures.</p></p>
