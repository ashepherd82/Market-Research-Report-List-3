<p><p>Formaldehyde purifiers available in the market come in various types such as HEPA (High Efficiency Particulate Air) and Ultra HEPA filters. HEPA filters are designed to capture particles as small as 0.3 microns, including formaldehyde molecules, effectively removing them from indoor air. Ultra HEPA filters, on the other hand, have an even higher efficiency in capturing particles down to 0.1 microns, making them more effective in purifying the air from formaldehyde and other harmful pollutants. Both types of purifiers are popular choices for improving indoor air quality.</p></p>
