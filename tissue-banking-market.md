<p><p>Tissue banking is the process of collecting, storing, and distributing human tissues for transplantation or research purposes. It involves the acquisition of tissues from living or deceased donors, followed by preservation and storage in specialized facilities. Tissues commonly stored in tissue banks include bone, skin, heart valves, corneas, and tendons.</p><p>The global tissue banking market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 7.00% during the forecast period. Several factors contribute to this growth, including an increasing prevalence of chronic diseases and a rising demand for transplantation procedures. Additionally, technological advancements in tissue preservation and storage techniques have improved the success rates of transplant procedures, further driving market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the growing number of tissue banks and centralized tissue processing facilities is boosting the market. Increased awareness about the benefits of tissue transplantation and rising healthcare expenditure are also driving the demand for tissue banking services.</p><p>In recent years, there have been several advancements and trends in the tissue banking market. Tissue engineering is gaining prominence as a potential solution for tissue shortage, with researchers exploring ways to grow tissues in the lab for transplantation. Furthermore, the integration of automated systems and robotics in tissue banking processes has improved efficiency, accuracy, and cost-effectiveness.</p><p>Another trend is the adoption of innovative preservation techniques such as cryopreservation, which involves freezing tissues at extremely low temperatures. This method has shown promising results in maintaining tissue integrity and viability during long-term storage.</p><p>Overall, the tissue banking market is expected to thrive in the coming years, driven by an increased demand for tissues for transplantation and advancements in storage and preservation techniques.</p></p>
