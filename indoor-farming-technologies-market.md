<p><p>Indoor farming technologies refer to techniques used to grow crops in an indoor environment, away from traditional outdoor farming methods. Aeroponics is a type of indoor farming where plant roots are suspended in air and nutrients are misted onto the roots. Hydroponics involves growing plants in water, with nutrients dissolved in the water. Other types of indoor farming technologies include aquaponics (a combination of hydroponics and aquaculture) and vertical farming (cultivating plants in vertically stacked layers). These methods provide controlled conditions, optimize resource utilization, and offer year-round crop production opportunities, reducing reliance on weather conditions and expanding agricultural possibilities.</p></p>
