<p><p>The automotive auxiliary brake market consists of two main types: drum brakes and disc brakes. Drum brakes use friction caused by brake shoes pressing against the inner surface of a rotating drum to slow down the vehicle. On the other hand, disc brakes use brake pads pressing against a disc to generate friction and stop the vehicle. Both types of brakes are essential components in vehicles for ensuring safe and efficient braking performance.</p></p>
