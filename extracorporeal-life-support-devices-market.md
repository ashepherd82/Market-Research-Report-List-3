<p><p>Extracorporeal Life Support (ECLS) devices, also known as extracorporeal membrane oxygenation (ECMO) devices, are mechanical devices that mimic the function of the lungs and/or heart to provide life support to patients suffering from severe respiratory or cardiac failure. These devices are designed to pump blood from the patient's body to an external artificial lung or heart, where it is oxygenated and then returned back to the patient's body.</p><p>The growth of the extracorporeal life support devices market is being driven by several factors. Firstly, there is an increasing prevalence of cardiovascular and respiratory diseases, such as acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS) and heart failure, which require life support treatment. Secondly, advancements in technology have led to the development of more advanced and efficient ECLS devices, which are able to provide better patient outcomes. Additionally, the growing geriatric population, who are more susceptible to these diseases, is also contributing to the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, there has been increasing adoption of ECLS devices in both developed and developing countries. This is attributed to the rising awareness among healthcare professionals about the benefits of ECLS therapy, as well as the availability of reimbursement for these devices in some countries. However, the high cost of these devices and the complexity associated with their usage are likely to hinder market growth to some extent.</p><p>Some of the latest trends observed in the extracorporeal life support devices market include the development of portable and miniaturized ECLS devices, which offer easier transportation and monitoring of critically ill patients. <p><p>Extracorporeal life support devices, specifically Venoarterial (VA) ECMO and Venovenous (VV) ECMO, are advanced medical technologies used to provide temporary mechanical support to the heart and lungs. VA ECMO supports both the cardiac and respiratory systems by pumping and oxygenating blood outside the body and then returning it to the patient's arterial system. On the other hand, VV ECMO primarily provides respiratory support by removing carbon dioxide and replenishing oxygen from the blood before returning it to the venous system. Both types of ECMO devices aid in the stabilization of critically ill patients in need of life-sustaining cardiac and/or respiratory support.</p></p>
