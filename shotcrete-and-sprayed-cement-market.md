<p><p>Shotcrete, also known as sprayed cement, is a construction technique that involves projecting concrete at high velocity onto a surface. It is utilized in a range of applications, including building repairs, slope stabilization, and underground constructions. Wet spraying is a shotcrete method where the concrete is mixed with water, while dry spraying involves using pre-mixed dry materials without water. Both methods offer advantages, such as enhanced bonding and reduced rebound, but the choice depends on project requirements and conditions. Wet spraying provides better workability and reduced dust, while dry spraying offers improved control and minimized material waste.</p></p>
