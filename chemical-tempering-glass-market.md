<p><p>Chemical tempering is a process where glass is strengthened through the use of chemicals. The glass is immersed in a potassium salt bath which replaces the smaller sodium ions in the glass with larger potassium ions. This creates a compressive stress layer on the surface of the glass, making it more resistant to breakage and scratches. Chemical tempering is known to increase the strength of the glass by up to four times compared to untreated glass.</p><p>The Chemical Tempering Glass Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.9% during the forecast period. <p><p>Chemical tempering glass is categorized into two main types: low temperature and high temperature. Low temperature chemical tempering involves heating the glass to a lower temperature range, typically around 200-400 degrees Celsius, and then treating it with chemicals to enhance its strength and durability. On the other hand, high temperature chemical tempering glass is processed at higher temperatures, usually above 500 degrees Celsius, to achieve similar benefits. Both types of chemical tempering offer increased resistance to impact and thermal stress, making them ideal for various applications.</p></p>
