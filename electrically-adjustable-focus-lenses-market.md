<p><p>Electrically adjustable focus lenses are used in various devices such as cameras, microscopes, and eyeglasses to provide adjustable focal lengths for clear image capture or viewing. There are two main types of electrically adjustable focus lenses available in the market, including electrowetting-based liquid lenses and liquid crystal lenses. Electrowetting-based liquid lenses use electricity to change the curvature of a liquid droplet, while liquid crystal lenses use electrical currents to alter the refractive index of the liquid crystal material, allowing for focus adjustment.</p></p>
