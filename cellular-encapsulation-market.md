<p><p>The cellular encapsulation market comprises various types of encapsulation materials, including alginate, chitosan, hydrogels, siliceous encapsulates, cellulose sulfate, and others. Alginate is a natural polysaccharide derived from brown algae, while chitosan is obtained from chitin, commonly found in the exoskeleton of crustaceans. Hydrogels are polymer networks that can hold large amounts of water, providing a favorable environment for cell growth. Siliceous encapsulates are made of silica nanoparticles, and cellulose sulfate is a modified form of cellulose. Other encapsulation materials may include synthetic polymers or biopolymers, offering different properties for cell encapsulation purposes.</p></p>
